🔄 4WD System — How to Engage & Disengage

Read before inspecting. The 4WD buttons are on the dash to the left of the steering wheel.

2H — 2-Wheel High
Normal everyday driving. Rear wheels only. Best fuel economy.
4H — 4-Wheel High
Slippery roads, mud, gravel. Use under 55 mph. OK to engage while moving slowly.
4Lo — 4-Wheel Low
Off-road, heavy pulling, steep grades only. Very slow speeds. Must be stopped to engage.
Auto 4WD
Automatically engages 4WD when slip is detected. Fine for mixed conditions.

How to Engage 4H:

  1. Slow the truck to under 55 mph or come to a stop.
  2. Press the 4H button on the dash. The 4H indicator light will illuminate.
  3. You may feel or hear a slight click as the transfer case engages — this is normal.
  4. Do not use 4H on dry pavement — it causes drivetrain binding and damage.

How to Engage 4Lo:

  1. Come to a complete stop. Place the transmission in Neutral.
  2. Press and hold the 4Lo button for 2–3 seconds until the 4Lo light illuminates.
  3. Place transmission back into Drive or Reverse. Maximum speed in 4Lo is 25 mph.

How to Disengage (Return to 2H):

  1. From 4H: Slow to under 55 mph or stop, then press the 2H button. Light will go off.
  2. From 4Lo: Come to a complete stop, place in Neutral, press 2H and hold 2–3 seconds until light goes off. Then shift back to Drive.
  3. If the 4WD light stays on after pressing 2H, drive forward slowly about 10–15 feet — this releases pressure and allows the transfer case to disengage.
⚠ Never use 4Lo or 4H on dry pavement. It causes drivetrain binding, excessive tire wear, and can damage the transfer case. 4WD is for low-traction surfaces only.
