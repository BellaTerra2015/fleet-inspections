🔄 4WD System — Manual Locking Hubs

Read carefully — this truck uses MANUAL LOCKING HUBS, not push button 4WD. Two steps required: lock the hubs AND move the transfer case lever.

2H — 2-Wheel High
Normal everyday driving. Hubs unlocked, lever in 2H. Best fuel economy.
4H — 4-Wheel High
Slippery roads, mud, gravel. Under 55 mph. Hubs locked + lever in 4H.
4Lo — 4-Wheel Low
Off-road, heavy pulling only. Must be stopped. Hubs locked + lever in 4Lo.
⚠ Important
Both steps must be done. Hubs alone or lever alone will NOT engage 4WD.

Step 1 — Lock the Front Hubs:

  1. Stop the truck completely on a safe surface.
  2. Get out and go to each front wheel. You will see a round dial in the center of each front hub.
  3. Turn the hub dial from FREE to LOCK — turn clockwise until it clicks into the LOCK position. Do both sides.
  4. Get back in the truck.

Step 2 — Move the Transfer Case Lever:

  1. With the truck stopped and transmission in Neutral, move the floor-mounted transfer case lever to 4H for normal 4WD use.
  2. For 4Lo: truck must be fully stopped, transmission in Neutral. Move lever all the way to 4Lo. Maximum speed 25 mph in 4Lo.
  3. Shift transmission back to Drive. Both hub locks and lever must be engaged for 4WD to work.

How to Disengage (Return to 2WD):

  1. Move the transfer case lever back to 2H while driving slowly or stopped.
  2. Stop the truck. Get out and turn both front hub dials from LOCK back to FREE.
  3. If hubs are left in LOCK while driving in 2H, it causes extra wear on front axle components. Always return hubs to FREE when 4WD is not needed.
⚠ Never use 4Lo or 4H on dry pavement. It causes drivetrain binding, excessive tire wear, and can damage the transfer case. 4WD is for low-traction surfaces only.
